Experience

Research Intern, National Institutes of Health

During summer 2018, I worked on computational genetics for osteosarcoma in Dr. Paul Meltzer's lab at the National Cancer Institute in Bethesda, MD. I analyzed next-generation sequencing data to search for large-scale structural rearrangements in the DNA sequences and improved the accuracy of a program to identify these mutations.

Software Engineering Intern, Commvault

During summer 2018 and 2017, I worked at Commvault, a company in Tinton Falls, NJ that makes enterprise data backup software. In 2018, I worked on creating CSV reports and feeding them into an ARIMA model to intelligently schedule background operations to not conflict with times of predicted user activity using C++. In 2017, I worked on creating a dynamic web UI to streamline the process of CI/CD using Angular, Java, and SQL.

Research Intern, Princeton University

During summer and fall 2016, I did research at Princeton's Andlinger Center for Energy and the Environment in Dr. Claire White's sustainable cements group. I analyzed pair distribution function data from the Advanced Photon Source particle accelerator to investigate the effects of sulfate attack on the nanostructure of alkali-activated slag cement, which has 98% fewer CO2 emissions than traditional cement.

Leadership

I'm very active on campus at Rutgers University and continually strive to make it a better place!

Vice President, Rutgers USACS

As VP of Education for Rutgers USACS (Undergraduate Student Alliance of Computer Scientists), I work on enhancing opportunities for the Rutgers tech community.

I spearheaded the new 1:1 mentorship program to match CS upperclassmen with underclassmen for holistic growth in academics, career/personal development, and technical knowledge, with over 100 mentees enrolled.

In addition, I run the Hacker Hour lecture series by students, alumni, industry representatives, and professors. I lead a team of students to find speakers and review their Hacker Hours for content and pedagogy.

I also collected student feedback and ideas on the CS curriculum and presented it to the department chair.

Senator, Rutgers University Senate

As part of the University Senate, I represent 20,000 School of Arts and Sciences students to the governing body of students, faculty, staff, and administrators.

As part of the undergraduate student government (Rutgers University Student Assembly), I work on practical initiatives to improve the student experience at Rutgers. These include advocating for affordability through providing free textbooks and clickers and enchancing career opportunities by creating a comprehensive alumni directory.

President, Rutgers Venture Capital Club

Venture Capital Club hosts speaker events, panel discussions, and trips to venture capital firms. We aim to educate students about the field, connect students to internships and jobs in venture capital, and provide Rutgers entrepreneurs with sources of funding.
